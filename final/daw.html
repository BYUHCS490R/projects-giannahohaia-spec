Key Terms

  • Tracks: Horizontal lanes for audio clips.
  • Plugins: Software effects (Equalization, Compression, Reverb, etc).
  • Automation: Pre-programmed changes over time.
  • Bouncing: Exporting the final file.

What is a DAW?

A Digital Audio Workstation (DAW) is the software platform used to record, edit, and mix audio. It replaces the large mixing consoles and tape machines of the past, and puts all the tools into a computer interface. Popular examples include Pro Tools, Logic Pro, Ableton Live, and FL studio. In the BYUH studio, we mostly use Pro Tools, but my personal favorite is Logic Pro.

Using a DAW gives you the ability to manage tracks, apply plugins, the use the mixer for volume and panning control.

Key DAW Terms

  • **Tracks:** Horizontal lanes for audio clips.
  • **Plugins:** Software effects (EQ, Comp).
  • **Automation:** Pre-programmed changes over time.
  • **Bouncing:** Exporting the final mixdown file.

What is a DAW?

A **Digital Audio Workstation (DAW)** is the software platform used to record, edit, and mix audio. It replaces the large mixing consoles and tape machines of past studios, centralizing all tools into a computer interface. Popular examples include Pro Tools, Logic Pro, and Ableton Live.

The core functionality of a DAW revolves around managing **tracks** (where audio is stored), applying **plugins** (effects), and utilizing the **mixer** (for volume and panning control).

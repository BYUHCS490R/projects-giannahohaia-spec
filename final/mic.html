Microphone Types

Each type is suited for different sound sources due to their internal mechanisms.

  • Dynamic: Best for loud, close sources (snare drum, loud vocals).
  • Condenser: Best for detailed, quiet sources (acoustic guitar, studio vocals). Requires (+48V) phantom power.
  • Ribbon: Warm, vintage sound, very fragile.

What is a Microphone?

The microphone is the first part of the signal flow. They convert acoustic energy into electrical energy. Choosing the right microphone is the most important decision an engineer can make before recording to ensure the highest quality results.

Polar Patterns

A polar pattern describes the sensitivity of a microphone to sound coming from different directions. It shows where the mic "listens" and where it rejects sound. Shure: Microphone Directionality and Polar Pattern Basics

The most common patterns are:

  • Cardioid (Heart): Sensitive to sound directly in front, rejects sound from the back.
  • Omnidirectional (Circular): Sensitive equally to sound from all directions.
  • Bidirectional (Figure-8): Sensitive to sound from the front and the back, rejects sound from the sides.

Key Terms

  • Pre-Delay: The short period of silence before the reverberation starts, which helps separate the original sound from the reflections.
  • Decay Time: The length of time it takes for the reverberation to fade completely.
  • Delay Time: The time interval between repeated echoes, often synchronized to the tempo of the song.

Reverb vs. Delay

Reverberation

Reverberation is the reflection of sound waves off surfaces in a physical space. It makes the listener feel like the sound exists in a real environment (hall, plate, room, etc.) Avixa: What is Reverb?

Delay

Delay is when an echo is played back after a short, specific period of time. This gives the illusion of depth, size, or rhythmic effect.

The time of the delay is often synchronized to the tempo of the song (quarter notes, eighth notes) to ensure it complements the rhythm and doesn't sound cluttered.

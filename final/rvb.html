Time-Based Effects

These effects create the illusion of a physical space.

  • **Pre-Delay:** Time before the first reflection is heard.
  • **Decay Time:** How long the reverb tail lasts.
  • **Feedback:** Controls the number of delay repeats.

Reverb and Delay: Creating Space and Depth

**Reverb** (Reverberation) is the complex reflection of sound waves off surfaces within a room. It makes the listener feel like the sound exists in a physical space (hall, plate, room, etc.).

In mixing, reverb is almost always applied using an **aux send/return** track. This ensures all elements share the same simulated acoustic space, which is a best practice for mix cohesion. Sweetwater: Aux Send and Return (Reference 5 of 5).

Delay

**Delay** is a simpler effect that plays back a copy of a sound after a short, distinct period. To ensure a tempo-synced delay sits perfectly within the song's rhythm, the engineer calculates the millisecond delay time by dividing 60,000 (milliseconds in a minute) by the song's Beats Per Minute (BPM), then multiplying by the desired fractional note.

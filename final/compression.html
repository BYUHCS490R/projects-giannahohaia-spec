What is Compression?

Compression is reducing the dynamic range of an audio signal, making the quietest parts louder and the loudest parts quieter. This results in a sound that is more consistent and controlled, which helps it sit better in a busy mix.

Source: Izotope by Native Instruments: What Is Audio Compression?

Four Parameters of Compression

  1. Threshold:The volume level at which the compressor comes in. This keeps volume consistent.
  2. Ratio: Determines how much the signal is reduced once it crosses the threshold.
  3. Attack: How quickly the compressor starts reducing gain. This controls the emphasis on the sound's transient (initial hit).
  4. Release: How quickly the compressor stops reducing gain.

Fast attack times are often used on drums (especially snares) to capture the transient quickly, while slower attacks allow the transient to pass through, preserving the punch. This timing is essential to the feel of the mix. Audio University Youtube: How To Use A Compressor

Frequency Guide

The human hearing range is around 20 Hz to 20 kHz.

  • Sub-Bass (20-60 Hz): aka "Rumble", felt more than heard.
  • Low Mids (250-500 Hz): Often responsible for "muddiness" in a mix.
  • Presence (2-4 kHz): Where vocals cut through the mix.
  • Air (10-20 kHz): Clarity and sparkle.

Source: The Mixing Engineer's Handbook, by Bobby Owsinski (Hal Leonard Books, 2021).

Equalization

Equalization (EQ) is the process of adjusting the balance of frequencies in a digital signal. We use EQ to enhance desirable parts of a sound and reduce harsh or muddy parts.

Engineers often use a High Pass Filter (HPF) to remove unnecessary low-frequency rumble (sub-bass) from instruments like vocals or acoustic guitar. This cleans up the low end of a mix, which is an important concept in mastering. Audio University: HPF Guide

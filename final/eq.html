Frequency Guide

The human hearing range is roughly 20 Hz to 20 kHz.

  • **Sub-Bass (20-60 Hz):** Rumble, felt more than heard.
  • **Low Mids (250-500 Hz):** Often responsible for "mud" in a mix.
  • **Presence (2-4 kHz):** Where vocals cut through the mix.
  • **Air (10-20 kHz):** Clarity and sparkle.

Equalization (EQ) Basics

Equalization, or **EQ**, is the process of adjusting the **balance of frequency components** within an electronic signal. We use EQ to enhance desirable parts of a sound and reduce undesirable, harsh, or muddy parts. [Image of a graphic equalizer interface with frequency bands]

Key Filter Types

A primary function of EQ is to create **spectral space** for each instrument. The most common filter used for cleanup is the High-Pass Filter (HPF).

Engineers often use an **HPF** to remove unnecessary low-frequency rumble (sub-bass) from instruments like vocals or acoustic guitar. This cleans up the low end of the entire mix, which is a key concept in mastering. Sweetwater: HPF Guide (Reference 3 of 5).
